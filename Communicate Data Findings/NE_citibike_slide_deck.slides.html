Factors affecting number of bike rentals, trip duration and the effect of COVID-19 pandemic on Citi Bike¶

by Nour Emam¶

Investigation Overview¶

Citi Bike is New York's offical bike sharing system. It consists of a fleet of bikes that are locked into docking stations spread out across the city. The goal of this investigation was to understand the profiles of different users of the bike rental platform, what could be the most common characteristics that would effect the duration of a single bike trip and what was the effect of COVID-19 on the usage of the bike rental platform.

Dataset Overview¶

The dataset was obtained via Bikeshare. It consists of just over 4 million records and 11 variables. Each record represents one trip and the data is monthly for the years 2019 and 2020. The variables that describe a trip include trip duration, start and end station, start and end date as well as some user specific data such as age, user type (can be a customer or a subscriber) and gender. 40,048 records (only around 1% of the data) were excluded from the analysis after the initial investigation of distributions due to the extremes in trip duration recorded which was causing inconsistencies.

Age Distribution¶

Citi Bike is most popular among users aged between 25-29 with around 750,000 trips followed by age groups 30-34. Third most common age group is 50-54 with just under 600,000 trips recorded across both years 2019 and 2020.

Gender vs Duration¶

Most male users rent the bikes for shorter durations (10 mins on average) compared to female bike riders (average slightly higher), the graph shows that females and mostly 'unknown' are distributed across higher durations. With 'unknown' having the highest mean duration of around 20 minutes.

User Type vs Duration¶

As for user types, most subscribers go for shorter trips since they are probably using Citi Bike more often throughout their day-to-day to get to work or school. While customers on the other hand tend to rent the bikes for longer durations with almost double the average duration of subscribers, this shows that customers might be tourists or users not premanently staying in New York so could be using the bikes for longer durations to explore the city.

Impact of COVID-19 on Number of Trips and Trip Duration¶

The next graph shows how in the beginning of 2020 the number of trips were higher in January and February than they were in 2019. However, those numbers fell in March 2020 compared to 2019 which marks the start of the first COVID-19 lockdown and continued to fall dramatically in April 2020. Number of trips continued to be less and looked to start to recover back to previous year's numbers by August. By September, 2020 numbers seem to have been higher which shows a recovery and continued to be higher than 2019 in the remaining months until the end of the year.

One Final Interesting Insight!¶

Even though the number of trips between March and August 2020 was lower than the same months in 2019, this graph shows that the average duration of trips was actually higher. This could indicate that due to lockdown when shops, work, schools and other facilities were closed, people would have had more time to rent bikes to enjoy the outdoors in a socially distanced activity.
